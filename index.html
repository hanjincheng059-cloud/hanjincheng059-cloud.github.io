Jincheng (Malcolm) Han

Historian of Global Trade, Medicine, and Empire

Ph.D. Student, Macau University of Science and Technology · Visiting Scholar, UCLA Department of History

I am a Ph.D. student in History at the Macau University of Science and Technology (MUST) and a Visiting Scholar in the Department of History at UCLA. My research sits at the intersection of global trade history, medical history, and the material culture of late imperial and modern China.

My doctoral dissertation examines Sino-Swedish trade in the Qing dynasty through the lens of the Canton Port (1735–1813). I also work on the global circulation of medicinal commodities — particularly China root (tufuling, Smilax glabra) — and how disease, commerce, and imperial institutions shaped early modern globalization. Drawing on European East India Company archives, Qing customs records, and Ming-Qing medical texts, I explore how the Qing empire engaged with the wider world under conditions of limited openness.

Interlude · 词一阕

三台 · To the Tune of San Tai (a Grand Suite)

After the model of Wan Sĭ Yong's San Tai (Song dynasty) — three stanzas, 171 characters, one rhyme throughout. A lyric on a historian's journey: from a year's-end birth, through long nights in the archives, to the sails of the Swedish East India Company.

记腊月雪残岁尽，玉漏亥初灯雨。
寅宫虎、将印两相扶，火六局、星屯武库。
禄随身、不竞少年步。
惯自扛、不言其苦。
望高处、岂逐凡流，有蓝图、未输今古。

惯孤馆青灯夜永，万卷暗藏归路。
三年灯、谁惜案头人，早看破、不甘迟暮。
破军动、火钺催帆橹。
趁潮信、便辞危浦。
海天宽、何处无师，有贵人、五洲烟树。

哥德堡千帆旧事，簿载茯苓茶贾。
广州潮、暗涌十三行，一笔笔、唤回掌故。
紫微星、终有称王处。
守得住、自成门户。
趁长风、再渡重洋，这青史、从头自谱。

Born as the last snows of the twelfth moon melted, at the very end of the year — lamplight and rain at the hour of Hai. In the Tiger's palace the General and the Seal stand guard together; a destiny cast in the sixth measure of Fire, stars garrisoned in the armory. Fortune follows me: I need not race the young. Used to carrying burdens alone, never speaking of their weight. Eyes on the heights — why follow the common stream? I hold a blueprint that yields nothing to past or present.

Long nights in a solitary study, the green lamp an old companion; ten thousand scrolls conceal the road onward. Three years of lamplight — who pities the one bent over the desk? Seeing through it early, I refuse to wait for dusk. The Army-Breaker stirs; fire and halberd hurry the oars. Riding the tide, I leave the perilous harbor. The sea and sky are wide — where is there no teacher? Benefactors wait among the mist-wrapped trees of five continents.

Gothenburg — old tales of a thousand sails; ledgers heavy with China Root and the merchants of tea. The Canton tide surges unseen through the Thirteen Hongs; stroke by stroke, the pen calls the past back to life. The Purple Star shall find its throne at last; hold fast, and a house of one's own will stand. Riding the long wind to cross the ocean once more — this history I shall score anew, from the very beginning.

注释 · Annotations

词牌 · The tune. 《三台》为唐教坊大曲，此依宋万俟咏《三台·清明应制》正体：三叠，一百七十一字，仄韵一韵到底（雨库步苦古／路暮橹浦树／贾故处户谱，俱属鱼模部）。San Tai is a Tang-dynasty grand suite (daqu); this lyric follows the standard form of Wan Sĭ Yong's Song-dynasty model — three stanzas, 171 characters, a single oblique-tone rhyme throughout.

首叠 · Stanza I. 「腊月雪残岁尽，玉漏亥初」——作者生于农历腊月三十亥时，岁之将尽而新岁未来。「寅宫虎、将印两相扶」：紫微斗数命宫在寅，武曲（将星）与天相（印星）同坐；「火六局」「禄随身」皆命盘术语，谓化禄入命，不必与人争一时之快。Born on the last night of the lunar year at the hour of Hai (9–11 p.m.), the poet opens with his natal chart in the Ziwei Doushu tradition: the General (Wuqu) and the Seal (Tianxiang) stand together in the Tiger palace, a destiny of the Fire-Six configuration, with Fortune (hualu) following the self — hence no need to race one's peers.

次叠 · Stanza II. 「破军动、火钺催帆橹」——迁移宫破军、火星、天钺三星会照，主远行与变革；「五洲烟树」谓天钺贵人散在四海。The second stanza turns to the scholar's long apprenticeship and the call to depart: the Army-Breaker (Pojun), the Fire Star and the Halberd (Tianyue) — stars of journey and transformation in the travel palace — hurry the oars toward benefactors waiting on five continents.

末叠 · Stanza III. 「哥德堡千帆」指瑞典东印度公司（1731–1813）；「簿载茯苓茶贾」谓其哥德堡拍卖账簿中之土茯苓（Radix Chinae）与茶叶买家——即作者所译注之 1733–1759 年销售档案；「十三行」为清代广州对外贸易之行商制度；「紫微星、终有称王处」用官禄宫紫微独坐之象，谓学问终成一家。末句「从头自谱」，既谓重谱青史，亦自况治史者重释世界之志。The final stanza fuses fate-chart and archive: the thousand sails of Gothenburg are the Swedish East India Company, whose auction ledgers — recording China Root and tea buyers, 1733–1759 — the poet has translated; the Thirteen Hongs were the licensed merchant houses of the Canton trade. The Purple Star (Ziwei) enthroned in the career palace promises a school of one's own; "to score this history anew" is both the translator's labor and the historian's ambition to re-narrate the world.
