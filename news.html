Research Notes

2026 · Los Angeles

On Material Approaches to Chinese History

Developing a framework at UCLA for studying late imperial Chinese history through its material remains — not just texts, but the objects scholars lived with: inkstones, brushes, devotional icons, and the physical books that circulated through commercial networks. The material turn offers a way past the text-centric bias of traditional Sinology.

2025 · Macau

On the Swedish East India Company Archives

Working through the SOIC records in Gothenburg (digitized). The Swedish company was a relative latecomer to the Canton trade (first voyage 1732), but its archives are remarkably well-preserved. What distinguishes the Swedish case from the British EIC is the absence of territorial ambition — the SOIC was purely commercial, which shaped its relationships with the Hong merchants differently.
